Blockchain in finance: A hit or miss?

Quickly evolving from the backbone of digital currency, blockchain integration within traditional financial products and services have resulted in an estimated growth of a whopping $43.1 billion by the year 2030. The global payments landscape has already taken a different shape as existing institutions follow suit, and third-party payments and financial service providers are investing heavily towards blockchain integration. From seamlessly supporting global payments processes, international trade and even regulatory compliance through AML processes, the possibilities are seemingly endless. The provisions of blockchain in new avenues such as supporting the creation and use of digital identities can further improve financial transactions, a stark contrast from the current role that traditional financial institutions play; being an intermediary to move payments between different entities. Numerous industries have been plagued with complex time-consuming processes, and it’s time that the world moves past limiting, conventional norms that end up in manual paperwork processing affecting efficiency. In countering financial crimes and avoiding data misrepresentation, where larger banking and financial institutions maintain multiple systems for customer records, blockchain ensures that current data and updates will be immediate on every system, enabled through an instant and concise audit trail. But beyond disciplining a traditional financial and banking system and streamlining processes, the ultimate question of where the overall future of banking and financial freedom ends up, arises. Limitations through status quo, tight regulations and the hassles and latencies of cross-border payments altogether can all be tackled with the support of blockchain to create a new, self-sufficient financial ecosystem of sorts. Digital assets could be used as a medium in itself to bring about a new realm of financial processes and freedom, established on the reliability and efficiency of blockchain. As a financing medium, the digital asset would be used as a prepaid card, debit and credit cards, and even taking it a step– or a lot of steps further by being able to use it for mortgaging, loans or for lending and borrowing. These products and services will even be made available for business purposes, where small business owners especially could benefit from the support, as opposed to traditional banking or financial institutions where it is deemed challenging to obtain funding or loans without proper backing or documentation. With this gaping market potential for individuals and business owners alike, there are vast opportunities to cater to the world in their financial journey to freedom.
